LinkedIn algorithm signals — what matters in 2026
Relevance beats volume
LinkedIn's 2026 algorithm rewards consistent relevance to a defined audience over high post frequency. Briskfil should own 3 to 4 core topics and repeat them. Plant engineers and procurement managers need to associate the brand with compressed air expertise before they ever have a buying need.
Early engagement is critical
The first 60 to 90 minutes after posting determine whether a post gets wide distribution. Saves and comments carry more weight than likes. Activate the Briskfil team to engage immediately after each post goes live. Two or three internal engagements in the first hour doubles reach consistently.
Company page plus people strategy
The strongest B2B LinkedIn presence in 2026 combines Company Page posts with personal posts from key team members. If the Briskfil founder or technical leads can share their own expertise, the combined reach significantly outperforms the Company Page alone.
Native content always wins
LinkedIn supresses posts that drive users off the platform. All carousels, videos, and images should be uploaded natively. Avoid leading with links. If a blog post or article is worth sharing, put the key insight in the post body and add the link as the first comment.

Short video program — 8-week brief for videographer

A structured production program built around Briskfil's core content themes. Each video is designed for LinkedIn native upload, 60 to 90 seconds maximum. Shot list and spoken brief included for each video. All can be filmed on site with a smartphone plus a lapel mic.

Week 1 — What we do: site visit walkthrough 60 sec

Objective: introduce Briskfil to a cold audience by showing the work, not describing it. No pitch. Pure process.

1
Opening — exterior of client facility
Camera: wide establishing shot of industrial site. No branding on site needed. Voiceover: "This is what a compressed air audit looks like in practice."
2
Shot — engineer connecting test equipment
Camera: close-up of equipment connection at pipe junction. Voiceover: "We start by connecting our particle count and moisture analyser directly to the distribution line."
3
Shot — reading the display
Camera: close-up of equipment readout or tablet display. Voiceover: "Within minutes we can tell you if your air meets ISO 8573 purity standards — or where it is failing."
4
Shot — engineer to camera
Camera: medium shot, direct to camera on site. Line: "Most clients are surprised by what we find. Compressed air problems rarely announce themselves — until they become expensive ones."
5
Caption for post
We spent the morning at a client site in northern Portugal. This is what 35 years of compressed air expertise looks like in practice. [Link in comments for a free air quality assessment.]
Week 2 — The cost of doing nothing: energy waste explained 90 sec

Objective: quantify the commercial pain of inaction. Hook the plant manager or financial director who controls the budget.

1
Hook — direct to camera, office setting
Line: "In most manufacturing plants, compressed air is the fourth biggest energy cost. Most finance directors have never looked at it." Hold 2 seconds. Cut.
2
Visual — diagram or whiteboard animation
Simple hand-drawn or filmed whiteboard: circle labelled "air system" with arrows to energy cost, productivity, and equipment life. Voiceover narrates each arrow briefly.
3
Stat card — on screen text
Text overlay: "A single 3mm leak in a compressed air line can cost over 1,500 euros per year." Hold for 3 seconds. Voiceover: "And most plants have more than one."
4
Close — engineer direct to camera
Line: "An audit pays for itself. We have seen clients recover the cost within three months of our first visit." Soft smile. Cut.
Week 3 — Client result: before and after 60 sec

Objective: social proof without a named client. Anonymised story format. Numbers are the hero.

1
Setup — narrator on camera
Line: "Last quarter we worked with a food manufacturing client in central Portugal. Their compressed air system looked fine on paper. It was not."
2
Finding 1 — text card or cut to site
Card or voiceover over site footage: "We found 7 leaks across the distribution network. Estimated cost: over 9,000 euros per year."
3
Finding 2 — continuation
Card: "Moisture contamination in the line was causing intermittent equipment faults. The client had been paying for repairs, not fixing the root cause."
4
Resolution — narrator
Line: "We fixed the system in two visits. The client saved more in the first year than they spent on five years of reactive maintenance. That is what we do."
Week 4 — Industry 4.0: what IoT monitoring actually looks like 75 sec

Objective: position Briskfil at the intersection of traditional expertise and smart factory technology. Attract a forward-looking audience.

1
Hook — provocative open
Line: "Industry 4.0 does not have to mean a complete factory overhaul. Sometimes it starts with knowing, in real time, exactly what your compressed air system is doing."
2
Demo — sensor installation footage
Camera: close-up of sensor being mounted on air line. Screen shows live data feed. Voiceover: "We install monitoring at key points in the distribution network."
3
Dashboard view
Camera: screen recording or screen share of monitoring dashboard showing pressure, flow, and particle count in real time. 5 seconds of live data scrolling.
4
Payoff
Line: "When something changes, you know immediately. Before it becomes a problem. Before it stops a production line. That is Industry 4.0 working for you."
