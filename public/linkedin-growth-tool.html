LinkedIn algorithm signals — what matters in 2026
Relevance beats volume
LinkedIn's 2026 algorithm rewards consistent relevance to a defined audience over high post frequency. Briskfil should own 3 to 4 core topics and repeat them. Plant engineers and procurement managers need to associate the brand with compressed air expertise before they ever have a buying need.
Early engagement is critical
The first 60 to 90 minutes after posting determine whether a post gets wide distribution. Saves and comments carry more weight than likes. Activate the Briskfil team to engage immediately after each post goes live. Two or three internal engagements in the first hour doubles reach consistently.
Company page plus people strategy
The strongest B2B LinkedIn presence in 2026 combines Company Page posts with personal posts from key team members. If the Briskfil founder or technical leads can share their own expertise, the combined reach significantly outperforms the Company Page alone.
Native content always wins
LinkedIn supresses posts that drive users off the platform. All carousels, videos, and images should be uploaded natively. Avoid leading with links. If a blog post or article is worth sharing, put the key insight in the post body and add the link as the first comment.

A structured production program built around Briskfil's core content themes. Each video is designed for LinkedIn native upload, 60 to 90 seconds maximum. Shot list and spoken brief included for each video. All can be filmed on site with a smartphone plus a lapel mic.

Week 2 — The cost of doing nothing: energy waste explained 90 sec

Objective: quantify the commercial pain of inaction. Hook the plant manager or financial director who controls the budget.

1
Hook — direct to camera, office setting
Line: "In most manufacturing plants, compressed air is the fourth biggest energy cost. Most finance directors have never looked at it." Hold 2 seconds. Cut.
