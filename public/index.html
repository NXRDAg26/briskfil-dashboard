Competition

Competitive intelligence for Briskfil — who they are, their weaknesses, and how to beat them

Five direct competitors · LinkedIn intel · Keyword opportunities

Five companies competing directly with Briskfil for clients, search rankings, and LinkedIn audience in the Portuguese and Iberian industrial gas market. Each analysis identifies their specific weaknesses and the exact positioning Briskfil can use to win.

Atlas Copco
Sweden · Global industrial giant · 807,000+ LinkedIn followers · Direct primary competitor
LinkedIn ↗ Primary threat
Global manufacturer of compressed air systems, industrial gas equipment, nitrogen and oxygen generators, and vacuum technology. Market leader across Europe and the primary competitor Briskfil clients compare against. 807,000+ LinkedIn followers posting 5+ times per week across multiple languages. Enormous brand recognition and budget. Their LinkedIn content mixes corporate culture posts (Water for All, DEI campaigns), product launches, and problem/solution carousels.
Their weakness
Corporate distance and committee-written content. Everything is filtered through brand guidelines and approval chains. Their posts read as marketing, not expertise. They sell their own products — they cannot offer independent advice. Their size means they cannot focus on the Portuguese market with any depth.
Briskfil's opportunity
Briskfil is what Atlas Copco cannot be: independent, Portuguese-speaking, locally accountable, and genuinely expert without a product range to push. Every Briskfil post that leads with independence and impartial expertise positions directly against Atlas Copco's commercial conflict of interest. Target their Portuguese-language search gap — they have near-zero Portuguese content.
Ultra Controlo
Sintra, Portugal · Est. 1987 · Medical oxygen, PSA systems, industrial compressors · 60+ countries
LinkedIn ↗ Portuguese rival
Portuguese manufacturer founded 1987 based in Sintra. Manufactures and supplies medical oxygen generators, PSA systems, vacuum plants, medical gas pipeline systems, and industrial compressors. Active in 60+ countries with increasing LinkedIn activity around medical oxygen and ISO 14001 sustainability. Content leans heavily on trade show appearances and product launches with emojis throughout.
Their weakness
Ultra Controlo has pivoted almost entirely to medical applications — medical oxygen for hospitals, clinics, and healthcare settings. Their industrial gas consultancy positioning has been abandoned. They are a product manufacturer with no independent consultancy offering. Their LinkedIn tone is corporate announcement style with no thought leadership.
Briskfil's opportunity
Briskfil owns the industrial gas expert positioning in Portugal that Ultra Controlo has vacated. Food, water treatment, wine, laser cutting, oil and gas, pharma — these sectors are underserved by Portuguese suppliers. Briskfil is the only Portuguese-based independent consultant serving them. This is direct whitespace.
Noxerior
Italy · PSA nitrogen and oxygen generators · Now part of Groupe Novair · 14 patents
LinkedIn ↗ Product manufacturer
Italian manufacturer specialising in non-cryogenic on-site nitrogen and oxygen generation using hollow-fibre membrane and modular PSA technology. Created the NITROSWING and OXYSWING product families. Acquired by French group Novair in 2016. 14 patents in modular PSA technology. LinkedIn activity is infrequent and product-announcement focused with minimal educational content.
Their weakness
Noxerior is a product company — they manufacture and sell systems. They cannot provide impartial technology selection advice because they only sell their own products. Post-acquisition by Novair their brand independence has diminished further. No Portuguese market presence or Portuguese-language content.
Briskfil's opportunity
The consultancy layer Noxerior cannot provide — helping buyers decide whether PSA or membrane is right for their application, comparing technologies from multiple manufacturers — is Briskfil's core value. Content on PSA vs membrane nitrogen generation that is genuinely impartial outranks Noxerior's product-led content in search and earns more trust from buyers.
Groupe Novair
France · World-leading on-site gas manufacturer · 4,500+ users · 150+ countries
LinkedIn ↗ Global manufacturer
French family-owned group founded 1977. Claims to be the world-leading manufacturer of on-site oxygen and nitrogen generators. Owns Noxerior, OGSI, and multiple other brands across medical, industrial, and pharmaceutical gas. Strong LinkedIn presence with product launches and global expansion announcements. Content is polished but entirely generic.
Their weakness
Novair has zero Portuguese market presence and zero Portuguese-language content. Their scale means messaging is never targeted to specific national markets or sectors. They manufacture products — they do not advise buyers on whether those products are right for them. Their content is produced from Paris for a global audience and lands nowhere with specific depth.
Briskfil's opportunity
Portuguese manufacturers searching in Portuguese for on-site gas generation solutions will not find Novair. They will find Briskfil — if Briskfil creates the content. This is the single biggest SEO gap in the Iberian industrial gas market. Own it before any of these manufacturers expands their Portuguese-language content.
CompAir
Germany · Ingersoll Rand division · 29,000+ LinkedIn followers · Founded 1801
LinkedIn ↗ Corporate manufacturer
Global compressed air manufacturer, founded 1801, now a division of NYSE-listed Ingersoll Rand. 29,000+ LinkedIn followers. Makes rotary screw, oil-free, portable, and centrifugal compressors plus air treatment products. High volume multilingual LinkedIn content — compressor launches, energy savings calculators, oil-free certification posts, iConn IoT monitoring content. Strong on sustainability messaging.
Their weakness
Every CompAir post is ultimately trying to sell a CompAir product. They have an inherent commercial conflict — their audits and recommendations are designed to lead to CompAir equipment sales. Buyers who have experienced misspecification from manufacturer-led assessments are highly receptive to independent alternatives. No Portuguese-language content strategy.
Briskfil's opportunity
Position directly against CompAir's conflict of interest. A compressed air audit from CompAir will recommend CompAir equipment. A Briskfil audit recommends whatever is right for the client's application — regardless of manufacturer. This message resonates strongly with procurement leads who have been burned by vendor-led recommendations. Target CompAir's audience directly with independence messaging.
Oxywise
Czech Republic · PSA oxygen and nitrogen generators · Export-focused · Medical and industrial
LinkedIn ↗ Product exporter
Czech manufacturer of PSA oxygen and nitrogen generators for medical, industrial, and aquaculture applications. Export-focused with European, Asian, and African market presence. LinkedIn content is infrequent and case-study heavy — medical oxygen for rural hospitals, nitrogen for specific industrial clients. Limited Iberian market presence. Corporate product-focused tone throughout.
Their weakness
Oxywise sells boxes. They have no consultancy layer — no independent assessment, no technology selection advice, no ongoing audit capability. Their relationship with a buyer ends at equipment delivery. No Portuguese-language content and no demonstrated understanding of the Portuguese or Iberian industrial market.
Briskfil's opportunity
Full-service end-to-end positioning versus product-only. Briskfil designs, specifies, manages installation, commissions, and then audits ongoing performance — none of which Oxywise offers. For Portuguese manufacturers who want a local partner managing their gas systems rather than a Czech export transaction, Briskfil is the only credible option.
